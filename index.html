A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the most revered scientists, educators, and statesmen of modern India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he hailed from a modest family. Despite financial challenges, his curiosity and determination drove him to excel academically, earning a degree in aerospace engineering.

Kalam began his illustrious career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a pivotal role in the development of India’s first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into orbit. His significant contributions to India’s defense sector include leading the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, which earned him national acclaim.

Dr. Kalam also served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, where he became widely admired as the "People's President" for his humility, vision, and dedication to inspiring youth. Known for his simplicity and passion for education, he authored several books, including Wings of Fire, which continues to motivate millions. Even in his final moments on July 27, 2015, he was delivering a lecture to students. His life remains a beacon of inspiration for generations to dream big and serve the nation.

Biographies

  • Wings of Fire (1999): An autobiography tracing his journey from humble beginnings to becoming India’s leading aerospace scientist.
  • Ignited Minds (2002): Focuses on the potential of India's youth and emphasizes the importance of dreaming big and working for national development.
  • Turning Points (2012): A sequel to Wings of Fire, reflecting on the pivotal moments of his life, especially during his tenure as the President of India.
  • My Journey (2013): A personal narrative of inspirational experiences from his life.
  • Transcendence (2015): Explores his spiritual journey and association with Pramukh Swamiji.
